Game Design Research

According to a recent Ipsos study, 48% of Americans love puzzles and puzzle games. 59% of respondents said it was calming, and 42% said it gave their brain a boost. One of the numerous approaches to a puzzle game would be to search for patterns and make logical choices in a crossword puzzle. We included three word puzzle games in this app. Filipino, crossword, and cryptogram

Types of Word Puzzles

Crossword Puzzle:

A crossword is a word puzzle that usually takes the form of a square or a rectangular grid of white- and black-shaded squares.

Cryptogram Puzzle:

A cryptogram is a word puzzle featuring encrypted text that the user decrypts to reveal a message of some sort.

Filippine Puzzle:

In a Filipino puzzle, the aim is to fill in words on the basis of given descriptions, which may or may not be cryptic . Unlike crossword puzzles and cryptograms , there are no intersecting solution words.

Game Design & Business Model

Snifx game design is based as usually newpaper crossword. Every day a new puzzle will be uploaded, and once the user opens the game and begins completing the challenge, the game will be paused and resumed later. You can play the puzzle anywhere, at any time. The game's business model is standard pretty much the norm.

Ads Model:

Free puzzles will be available for play, and advertisements will appear while playing.

Premium Model:

Users that pay for the Premium mode will not see any advertisements and will only experience unlimited puzzles, community, create collection and download for offline gameplay.

Sponsor Model:

The sponsor will foot the bill to produce a brand-based puzzle using their brand puzzle.

Branding

The purpose of the Snifix branding was to provide the several games—Wordscapes, Scrabble, and Wordle—a more cohesive appearance while maintaining distinctive features that made each game easy to identify.

In order to draw more attention to the text, I concentrated on a pastel aesthetic and selected a bold font for the main text. I also added smaller, hand-drawn elements like puzzle pieces, blocks, and hand-drawn and hand-lettered layouts.

UI Design

A crossword puzzle game typically has a simple and intuitive user interface (UI) that is designed to make it easy for players to interact with the game. The game board, which is made up of a grid of squares, is typically the centerpiece of the UI. Each square in the grid can contain a letter, a blank space, or a clue. The game's UI should be designed in such a way that it makes it easy for players to navigate and interact with the game, while also providing them with all the information they need to solve the puzzle. The design should be simple and uncluttered, with a clear and consistent layout to minimize confusion and make the game more enjoyable to play. Overall, a crossword puzzle game's UI should be designed to provide a great user experience to players and help them stay focused on solving the puzzle.
